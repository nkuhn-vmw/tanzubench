# Biography: Dr. Carla Mendes

Carla Mendes was born on April 3, 1971, in São Paulo, Brazil, to a schoolteacher mother and an electrical engineer father. She showed early aptitude for mathematics and was admitted to the University of Campinas at sixteen on a merit scholarship, graduating in 1993 with a degree in applied mathematics.

In September 1993 she moved to Edinburgh, Scotland, to begin a doctoral program in computational fluid dynamics at the University of Edinburgh. Her thesis, completed in 1997, modeled turbulence propagation in industrial heat exchangers and earned a commendation from the faculty review board. During her Edinburgh years she met her future research collaborator, Dr. Fiona Aldridge, then a postdoctoral fellow in the same department.

Immediately after defending her dissertation in June 1997, Mendes relocated to Boston, Massachusetts, where she had accepted a research scientist position at Meridian Applied Science, a privately held engineering consultancy. She spent four years there working on simulation software for aerospace clients, publishing seven peer-reviewed papers and earning two patents for turbulence modeling algorithms.

In 2001 she was recruited by the mechanical engineering faculty at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York, where she was appointed assistant professor. She was promoted to associate professor in 2006 and full professor in 2011. Her Cornell lab, the Computational Dynamics Group, grew to include twelve graduate students and secured sustained funding from the National Science Foundation and two Department of Energy grants.

A pivotal career shift occurred in 2015 when Mendes accepted a dual appointment as a senior research fellow at the Alan Turing Institute in London, England, spending two months per year in London while maintaining her Cornell position. The Turing fellowship was renewed twice and expired in 2021.

In 2022, following the death of her longtime collaborator Dr. Aldridge, Mendes took a one-year sabbatical to write a book on numerical methods for engineers. She returned to Cornell in 2023 as the Aldridge-Mendes Chair in Computational Science — a named professorship endowed by a group of former students in honor of both researchers.

As of 2025, Mendes holds 14 patents, has supervised 31 PhD students to completion, and serves on the editorial board of the Journal of Computational Physics. She lives in Ithaca with her partner and their two dogs.
